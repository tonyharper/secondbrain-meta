# Additional Context on the Change Today

A few things to follow up on Michel's [post](https://fb.workplace.com/groups/2468134800109855/permalink/4458170867772895/) announcing our shift to a flatter operating model organized around groups:

First, there are no role eliminations. **Everyone still has a job.**

Second, to make it easier to operate, we're **mapping a specific individual to each Risk Group** as the primary owner / point person for coordination, day-to-day decisions, and escalation. These owners will help make sure questions land with the right person quickly, and that work has clear follow-through. Namely:

- Areas Response Platform: Risa Kawai
- Risk Review System & Platform: Erin Kim
- GRC Platform: Anastasia Gandrik
- Data-related Risk: Tony Harper
- User Risk: Ji Dong
- Youth: Jim Liu
- AI Risk / Monetization Risk: Connie Lun
- Agent Oversight: Anthony O'Sullivan

(Anthony will also be the primary owner / point person for Privacy Infra.)

Third, there are **no changes to top-level reporting lines** (e.g. DS and DE remain distinct, so Anastasia and Anthony still report to Tony, Ji still reports to Connie, etc.). With that said, to make it easier for ICs to operate day-to-day and to ensure they can be supported by managers, the following changes will happen within Established Risk:

- Alice Berthaux, Barnaby Cooper, David Alexander Morrison, Jenny Pei, Kedar Patil, and Konstantin Shamruk will now be supported by Ji Dong.
- Sebastian Poehlmann (and team), Wan Jiang and Xiaojun Shi will be supported by Baris Cem Sal.

I'm excited about the move to this flatter model. It will help Risk Leads get closer to the work, weigh in earlier, and provide clearer direction when needed. It will ensure key topics have dedicated focus and accountability, so decisions are faster and teams spend less time litigating (and re-litigating) strategic questions. **There's still also a lot we need to learn from making these changes** (notably, the right way to ensure Research is represented. I look forward to working with Chad Kiewiet de Jonge, Nihan Brunton, and Ashley Tallevi to figure out the right approach once Jackie Chan joins us in April).

Analytics & Research remains a critical horizontal function in this operating model. Our objective is to provide an accurate, insights-based view of reality so we can ensure Risk makes high-quality decisions, drive accountability, and tell the truth. Our role remains the same, and our work continues to be foundational to a well-run Risk org.
