# What the Risk Org Changes Mean for Established Risk

Michel's [post](https://fb.workplace.com/groups/2468134800109855/posts/4458170867772895/) today shares changes to how the Risk org will operate going forward. I want to give you the full picture of what this means for us in Established Risk.

**TLDR:** To ensure clear accountability and remove layers of decision making, the Risk org will move to a flatter operating structure of risk groups. Established Risk will no longer operate as a single pillar, and teams will align to two risk groups: **Data Risk** (accountable lead: Arash Nikkar) and **User Risk** (accountable lead: Jackie Zajac). The work today is the same as it was yesterday, but with a streamlined connection to a single accountable Risk leader to unblock teams quickly.

## What does this mean in detail?

The teams that have been part of Established Risk will align as follows:

### Data Risk

This includes teams within the User & Restricted Data, Competition and parts of the D8N group, covering risk areas including:

- Restricted Data
- User Data Inventory
- Anonymization
- Data Access & Portability
- Data Deletion & Retention
- Anti-Scraping
- Data In / Data Out
- Purpose Limitation
- Cross-Product Data Practices
- Competitive Data Use
- Cross-Promotion

Arash Nikkar is the Accountable lead for this area, and I will be DRI.

### User Risk

This includes teams within the Transparency & Choice group, Accessibility and News, covering risk areas including:

- Transparency
- Choice
- Profiling, Personalization & Automated Decision Making (PPA)
- Accessibility
- News

Jackie Zajac is the Accountable lead for this area. Bede McCarthy is DRI for News & Accessibility, Dave Nolan is DRI for T&C Controls and Cedric Lendais is DRI for T&C Decisions.

These alignments reflect the work teams are already doing -- this is formalizing existing focus areas, not moving people around. If you're working on data-related risk today, you're in Data Risk. If you're working on transparency, choice, accessibility, or news, you're in User Risk.

## What's a DRI? What's an Accountable lead?

Under the new model, each risk group has:

- A **DRI** -- the person driving strategy and execution across the group. The DRI is empowered to make decisions on behalf of the Accountable leader, and to know when to pull them in. They spend the majority of their time focused on ensuring the group is successful and unblocked.
- An **Accountable lead** -- the person accountable to Michel and Risk Leads for outcomes, providing oversight and serving as the escalation point.

## The work hasn't changed but how we work needs to

**What we do is the same.** We identify and mitigate risk, we drive fast and calibrated risk decisions, we lead regulatory response, and we continue maturing our controls and requirements. Our policies, compliance standards, and legal obligations are all the same.

**What changes is how we'll deliver that work.** Fewer layers of reviews, clearer ownership, faster decisions. Accountable leaders and DRIs who own each group end-to-end -- which means we can move faster on AI-native transformation and push the company forward. You can expect to see fewer meetings, more maker time, and more focus on being nimble and moving assertively to fully leverage AI.
