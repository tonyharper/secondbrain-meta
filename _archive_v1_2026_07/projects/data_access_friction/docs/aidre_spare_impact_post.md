# AIDRE & SPARE's Impact on Data Warehouse and Meta-Wide Friction Reduction

Author: Shruti Tyagi | Posted: 2025-12-23

## Executive Summary

The AIDRE team, in partnership with the SPARE (Security & Privacy Access Rebalancing Effort) initiative, has delivered transformative results in reducing Tech FTE data access friction across Meta. This update highlights the measurable impact on the Data Warehouse (DW) — historically responsible for ~50% of company-wide friction — and the broader Meta ecosystem.

## Key Points

- AIDRE's Data Warehouse work resolved Meta's #1 friction source.
- SPARE's company-wide approach enabled sustainable, secure, privacy-conscious data access.
- Accessmate is now the flagship investment for data access orchestration.

## The Challenge: Data Access Friction at Meta

- **Persistent Issues:** Data access at Meta was previously characterized by frequent disruptions, lengthy approval processes, and permission bottlenecks. These challenges created significant obstacles for employees and teams, often resulting in delays and frustration.
- **Employee Impact:** These challenges were consistently cited as top concerns in internal surveys, affecting productivity and satisfaction.

## The Solution: Accessmate & SPARE

### Accessmate - Unified AI Data Access

- **Centralized Approach:** Accessmate streamlines and automates data access, integrating with Daiquery, Scuba, Bento, Unidash, and Dataswarm.
- **Orchestration:** Manages DI data access capabilities including Managed ACL, Role-Based Access Control (RBAC), and Partial Data Preview (PDP) directly within user workflows.
- **Automation:** Guides users and automates approvals for faster, more reliable access.

### SPARE - Meta-Wide Friction Reduction

- **Program Launch:** SPARE began in late Q3 to create a sustainable, low-friction environment for secure and privacy-conscious data access across Meta.
- **Acceleration:** SPARE expedited the deployment and uptake of Accessmate, resulting in significant and quantifiable enhancements.

## Results: Significant Reduction in Friction

### Data Warehouse (DW) Impact

**Centralized Access Management:**
- 75% of Hive and Scuba ACLs, and 94% of Unidash ACLs are now managed through Accessmate, with ongoing efforts to onboard remaining ACLs and new ones using established processes.
- LBAC (Label Based Access Control) protections for high-risk Hive tables have reduced risk with minimal query interruptions, using an automated framework that adapts to updated ratings.

**Interruption Reduction:**
- Data access interruptions across analytics tools like Daiquery, Scuba, and Bento reduced by 50% (3.5% to 1.6%), surpassing H2 p90 goals (1.75%).
- Role-based access controls (RBAC) and improved configurations have streamlined user experiences.
- Privacy controls and requirements — including the removal of PZ terminal access checks, DAPR for non-Restricted Data queries, the adoption of Data Subject Type in User Data Protection, and the UDP Aggregated Check — were refined to reduce avoidable interruptions and streamline user experience.
- Partial data preview (PDP) features have further lowered interruptions for eligible queries.

**Data Warehouse Goals for SPARE:**
- Total interruptions: Down 65% (3.88m to 1.36m)
- Requests taking >1 day: Down 57% (94.7k to 40.8k)
- Friction hours: Down 48.5% (359k to 185k)

### Meta-Wide Friction (SPARE Goals)

The launch of SPARE drove a step-change in Tech FTE access efficiency, delivering a ~33% net reduction in friction — even as the workforce expanded by ~10%. The SPARE Initiative not only met, but **exceeded ambitious targets across all goal metrics**, outperforming 2024 baselines adjusted for workforce growth.

**Key Highlights:**

- **48% Reduction in Extended Requests**
  - Requests exceeding one day dropped from 164K to 85K (-48%).

- **33% Net Reduction in Data Access Friction**
  - Total friction hours decreased from 680K to 455K (-33%).
  - Productivity per Tech FTE rose by 33.6%, with friction hours per FTE dropping from 10.7 to 7.1.

## Conclusion

Friction reduction is now a reality — Data Access Friction is considered resolved.
