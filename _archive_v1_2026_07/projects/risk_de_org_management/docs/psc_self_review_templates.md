# Risk DE PSC Self-Review Templates

**Author:** Tony Harper
**Date:** 2025-06-17

Hi everyone - the H1 2025 Performance@ cycle kicked off last week. The self-review template hasn't changed much since the past, though there's now an 800-word limit that applies cumulatively across all sections (up from 600 or so words in the past).

As you fill out your self review, I want to encourage you to focus on framing up your impact and contributions in the first section of this template. Within the 800-word limit, you should probably be spending a majority of those words on your impact and contributions (section 1). As a rough rule of thumb, we recommend a 650/150 word split between the sections, but feel free to modify as you see fit.

- **[650+ words recommended] What impact have you achieved so far?**
- **[~150 words recommended] What are 1-3 strengths demonstrated (work you most enjoyed and excelled at)?**
- **[Does not count towards word limit] What are 1-3 areas you are intentionally working on this year to grow or improve?**

Historically, we've asked you to use a DE-specific template in your self-reviews to help you describe the unique work we do as DEs in the most understandable way possible to support high-quality calibrations.

Given the changes to the Meta template this half, we're amending our DE template to provide guidance on the first section. As with the previous template, we encourage you to **focus on impact, contributions, and evidence**. In sections 2 "Strengths" and section 3 "Development areas", please anchor on the specific DE expectations for your level.

In the first section, "Highlight your impact...", we encourage you to use a modified version of the previous DE PSC Self-Review Template, as seen below. This doesn't need to be a long list of things; it should be a clear, concise description of what was achieved, how you achieved it, and any evidence that you have of that.

## Template

> **Outcome 1**
>
> **Context and Impact**
> Brief description of the project/outcome and the impact **resulting from your contributions**. If the overall project had X impact, help us understand what part of that is attributable to your contributions, and how.
>
> **Contributions**
> What were your personal, concrete contributions to this outcome?
>
> **Evidence**
> From weakest to strongest. The stronger the better.
>
> - *Anecdotal:* [Your personal, direct observation: Weak]
> - *Testimonial from DE:* [The direct observation of a DE counterpart: Solid]
> - *Testimonial from XFN:* [The direct observation of an expert or group: Solid]
> - *Measurable/Documented:* [A verifiable measurement, empirical observation, or clear documentation: Strong]

As before, usage of this template is recommended but optional. However, we've found this template provides the best possible information to help your manager represent your work in calibrations.

If you have any questions, please reach out to me. Thank you!
