## What are my personal objectives in Q4 2025?

- **Reducing data access friction.** Privacy controls unfortunately made it such that it became quite difficult for people to get access to the data they needed to do their jobs, a point which came up in our Dev/Data Experience surveys over the past year or two. A joint Privacy/Security/Data Infra program (SPARE) was formed to deal with this, and I've been leading its data team for the past several months. We are now almost to a point where it's "good". One last push in Nov/Dec should get us over the line, after which we'll treat this as a guardrail.
- **Finding the Quality Bar for Risk data.** I'll have a lot more to say here in person, but our data quality isn't great. We acknowledge this, have talked about it extensively, have a maturity model that shows where we are and where we're going - but this has not been enough to build trust with XFN. We've also struggled to meet XFN expectations in terms of launch velocity for data and dashboards, and I feel that we cannot keep up this velocity of new data and quickly fix our quality at the same time. We need to figure this out without agreeing to do the impossible. There may well be a better balance to strike here than where we are currently, but slowing to a crawl to make sure our data is perfect is almost certainly not it.
- **Retention Policy Boundary for Analytics.** Earlier this year I co-wrote (primarily with PM) our Retention policies for the data warehouse, getting a very useful carveout for analytics. I now need to write the policy that defines analytics and get that landed so that we can apply the policy to the rest of the data warehouse.
- **Landing a small reorg in Risk DE.** I chose not to make changes to my org after the recent Privacy Areas reorg, instead choosing to read in my M2s and get their input on the future state. I am currently in the process of reviewing my tweaked org design with XFN and intend to land it in November. I'll tell you more about this when you're ready.
- **Performance@ Training.** I lead Performance@ training for the combined Risk/Facebook/MRS DE org. This includes formal training, workshops, and office hours where we review and give feedback on the clarity of Performance packets ahead of calibrations. It's a big lift but has helped to produce better quality outcomes. You would of course be welcome to attend this in December!
