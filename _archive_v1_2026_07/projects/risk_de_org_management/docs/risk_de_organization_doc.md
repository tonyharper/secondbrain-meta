# Risk DE Organization

## A Bit of Context

Risk DE is the amalgamation of four organizations what were distinct at the start of 2023:

- Privacy Foundations DE - from Infra
- Privacy Areas DE - from Central Products
- Data Understanding DE - a small org I had created in Facebook
- Central Youth DE - from two teams in Central Products

Foundations and Areas were brought together in May 2023 after a set of brutal layoffs where about 40% of the team was let go. I worked with the team as an advisor starting then, and officially started supporting the team in October 2023 after I came back from Recharge.

The combined org was definitely underperforming, and we were quite harsh during the end of year Performance Cycle. I managed out about half of the remaining team, and as such 2024 was a rebuilding year. I replaced most of the departed team members with senior DEs and managers with whom I have worked for years. Central Youth joined us recently, in April 2025.

### Pillars, Briefly

I'm about to make a few changes to the team; this is what it will look like after those. Descriptions of each pillar below.

- **Foundations** - builds data describing our core Risk products. Primarily focused on Risk Review and the Compliance Graph. Mostly responsible for building out the Flywheel (which you'll be hearing a lot about!). Mostly based in MPK, but with some NYC and SEA presence.
- **Existing Risk and Novel Risk** - formerly known as Privacy Areas. This is the "go do stuff" part of Risk, where we go build / change systems to meet compliance obligations. Mostly based in London, but with some NYC and MPK presence. Historically I have spent much of my time here, as this is where the big data warehouse system changes mostly take place.
- **Decision Engine** - the place where we make decisions about risk. Probably the most important piece of the puzzle right now, IMO. If we get it wrong here, we get it wrong everywhere. Small team, mostly in MPK.
- **Central Youth** - Like the Risk teams, but focused on Youth. Also includes our Age Modeling team. Mostly in MPK but with a large NYC presence.
- **Privacy Aware Infrastructure.** Not in Michel's org but closely allied. Their analytics team is supported by us. They build the infra for the Risk Areas. Historically a very tough area for analytics to support. Mostly in MPK.
- **Data Understanding.** Still a small distinct org in my team, but sits with PAI in the org chart.

### Team Strengths

- **System thinking and solution building.** We have a collection of DEs who think about systems - in particular the data warehouse but also Risk entirely - and are good at changing entire systems when we're able. We've done this primarily with the warehouse so far.
- **Direction within "Offline" Privacy.** Data Warehouse privacy (aka Offline Privacy) is in almost all cases very clearly our domain. Existing / Novel Risk teams listen to us and in many cases we design and build the solutions here.
- **Data Understanding.** "What data is in scope for Privacy?" is an age-old problem at Meta. Our team solved this for the data warehouse and will be extending this to other systems over time. This is the achievement that I'm most proud of from the past few years.
- **Direction/Collaboration within Analytics.** Tackling Meta-wide Privacy and Youth problems has meant setting direction across numerous analytics teams and helping them achieve goals alongside us. We've built strong relationships here and are pretty good at getting stuff done across analytics.

### Team Growth Areas

- **Data Visualization.** Not our strong suit. We would like to change this, but we're not there yet.
- **Saying No / Pushing Back.** With very limited exceptions (mostly our senior DE leaders), this team doesn't like to say no to anything. This harms both work/life balance and data quality.
- **Direction within Product.** Product teams listen to us when we're talking about things like data delays or quality issues, but we have effectively no voice when it comes to the product itself, with exceptions around data warehouse controls.
- **Ownership of Outcomes/Key Results.** With some exceptions, Data Engineering does not own any analytics or product outcomes. This is not for lack of desire, and in places where I am directly present I can usually get a few things with our names next to it. But generally, teams see us as a service organization even though we don't operate as one. This is directly related to the bullets above.
- **Seniority.** We are on average less senior than DS and a LOT less senior than our SWE/PM counterparts. This directly impacts the bullets above. IMO the scope is there for us to play a larger role, so I'd like to change this through deliberately growing our top talent.
