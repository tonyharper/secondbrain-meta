# 2025 Performance@ Cycle and AI Impact

**Author:** Tony Harper
**Date:** 2025-12-02

I've recently fielded questions about how AI usage will be viewed in the upcoming Performance@ cycle, and I want to clarify how we'll be recognizing and rewarding impact -- especially as it relates to AI.

**1. Impact through AI will be rewarded.** If you're leveraging AI to drive results -- whether that's through automation, smarter data analysis, or by improving team performance -- those contributions will be recognized and rewarded in this cycle. We're seeing incredible potential in how AI can amplify our work, and I want to make sure that impact is visible and valued.

Adoption of AI tooling alone doesn't qualify as impact, just as using SQL or Python alone doesn't create impact. That said, if you are learning this tooling, you are setting yourself up for 2026 - see #4 below on that point.

**2. All forms of impact matter.** AI is a powerful tool, but it's not the only way to make a difference. As always, we'll continue to reward the full spectrum of impact: technical excellence, collaboration, direction, and the many other ways you move the Risk DE mission forward.

**3. No penalty for not using AI in 2025.** I want to be clear: there is no penalty for not using AI in this cycle, and AI adoption metrics will not be used when determining ratings. If AI isn't the right fit for your work, or you're still learning, that's okay. We're in a period of exploration and learning, and I want everyone to feel comfortable experimenting at their own pace.

**4. Looking ahead to 2026: AI will be an expectation.** That said, expectations are evolving. By 2026, using AI productively will be a baseline expectation for everyone at Meta. I fully expect this expectation to be reflected in the 2026 Performance@ mid- and end-of-year cycles. I encourage you to start experimenting now -- learn the tools, try new workflows, and share what you discover.
