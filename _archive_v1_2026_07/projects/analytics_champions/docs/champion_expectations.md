# Expectations for Analytics Champions

Process overview: [Analytics/DI Collaboration](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1L6B2dr3Wi8uIggDN1fSNpel7t2WUy5T6uczZygd95Uc/edit#heading=h.p2um3c53qbzn)

Core responsibility: Act as a single voice representing their Analytics pillar's needs to the DI organization.

*Analytics/DI Champions are accountable for collecting, organizing, and prioritizing their pillar's DI needs. They are responsible for owning the process to do so, which should provide input from their pillar's leadership, DE/DI SMEs, and other stakeholders in order to appropriately represent the business needs of the pillar.*

NOTE: While each DI category might have multiple SMEs, it is the responsibility of the Analytics champion to identify a single POC for each domain to serve as the SME STO. Reference [this list](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1RJkdDOTp5I1o3BWt8Vg2RSatX6ZrXUDaqJLLqMWTrVE/edit#heading=h.m3ethv73u6wz) of domains and SME STOs.

## Identifies SME and establishes clear success criteria for their SME group

- Owns the process by which SMEs are identified and vetted for collaborating with DI. The SME group should align with the Analytics pillar's top priorities.
- Establishes roles and responsibilities for SMEs. Holds SMEs STOs accountable to the outcomes/objectives of their respective DI areas. Empowers SMEs to prioritize their DI/Analytics contributions.

## Enables execution across SME group

- Establishes pillar-level processes to ensure that SME group is driving the execution for their DI area(s) (e.g. regular syncs, short-term prioritization, etc)
- Serves as an escalation point for their SMEs and DI counterparts. Drives resolution to any issues/blockers that arise.
- Empowers SMEs and DI teams to re-assess and pivot strategy/priorities to ensure teams are focusing on the most important work and right set of problems.

## Drives holistic thinking and approach on Analytics workflows and lifecycles

- Considers and establishes end-to-end analytics lifecycles across multiple domains (whereas SMEs focus on a single domain, Champions bring together multiple domains to create the holistic vision)
- Identifies opportunities for Analytics and Analytics efficiency through better workflows
- Aligns the workflows with SMEs of relevant domains

## Advocates for and influences what gets prioritized across Analytics and DI

- Creates and owns process(es) by which feedback and inputs are collected, organized, and prioritized across their Analytics pillar
- Participates in the semi-annual Analytics Summit, providing both their pillar's top priorities as well as any papercuts identified in the process.
- Uses those inputs to review, align, and prioritize 'globally'. Advocates for and influences what gets prioritized across DI. The outcomes of this process ultimately feed into the final 'roadmap review' phase with DI and Data leadership.

## Brings awareness and visibility to the DI/Analytics work being done across their pillar

Acts as a voice and advocate for DI too (not just Analytics):

- Regularly communicates progress and updates across their pillar. Recognizes work/accomplishments of DI.
- Sets up pillar-level 'rhythms' (brownbags, hackathons, etc.) to showcase progress, promote collaboration, and identify opportunities.
- Captures feedback throughout half that can be used during cycle-level planning and prioritization.
- Looks for opportunities to further the DI/Analytics collaboration model and partnerships (e.g. new SMEs and contributors from Analytics)

## Participates in and contributes to the improvement of the overall process

- Proactively identifies opportunities to improve the overall Analytics/DI collaboration model; provides feedback to appropriate Analytics and DI leads; and drives the implementation of those improvements
- Engages and builds relationships with other key contributors (e.g. other Analytics champions) and stakeholders
- Participates in regular rhythms set up for Analytics champions (brings something to the table)
