# Analytics/DI Collaboration - Operating Structure, Roles, and Objectives

This document outlines the roles and responsibilities, objectives, owners, and meeting cadences for the collaboration between the Analytics and Data Infrastructure (DI) teams for the year 2024 to achieve the following objectives (Reference: [Evolving the Data Infra and Analytics Partnership](https://fb.workplace.com/notes/753887956554802)):

1. Define clear communication mechanisms, feedback channels, and directly responsible individuals across DI and Analytics community. (see Objectives 1 & 2)
2. Align on the top problems to solve, what good outcomes look like, and how we plan to measure success with clearly defined metrics. (see Objective 4)
3. Communicate decisions and share related information across Analytics, DI and our partners. (see Objective 3)

## Operating Structure

Source: [Tiered Accountability](https://www.internalfb.com/excalidraw/EX182475)

In the operating model between DI and Analytics organizations, there are four key roles:

1. **Analytic Champion:** This role is responsible for advocating for the needs and priorities of the Analytics team, ensuring their representation in strategic decisions. Given the broader data infrastructure needs of DE we have a champion per product pillar; DS has more narrow data infrastructure needs and can be represented by a single champion for the function.

2. **SME Program Lead:** There are two SME Program Leads, one from the Analytics organization and another from the DI organization. They oversee the formation and operation of SME groups, ensuring they are working effectively towards their goals.

3. **SME Group Lead:** There are five active domains (as of Feb'24) - Privacy & Security, Metric Trust & Data Governance, Warehouse as a Product, Data Consumption, and Experimentation (e.g. Deltoid). Machine Learning and GenAI for Analytics is under evaluation. The SME Group Lead is responsible for leading a specific SME domain, guiding them in their work and ensuring they meet their objectives.

4. **SME (Subject Matter Expert):** SMEs are the experts in their respective domains. They provide the technical knowledge and expertise necessary for the execution of projects and initiatives.

## Roles and Responsibilities

### Analytic Champion

**Responsibilities:**
- Act as the representative for their Analytics pillar's needs to the DI organization.
- Identify and vet SMEs for collaboration with DI.
- Establish Analytics pillar-level processes to ensure SME group execution for their DI domain(s).
- Establish end-to-end analytics lifecycles across multiple domains.
- Advocate for and influence what gets prioritized across Analytics and DI. Act as a voice and advocate for DI too (not just Analytics).
- Regularly communicate progress and updates across their pillar.
- Proactively identify opportunities to improve the overall Analytics/DI collaboration model.
- For details on expectations of Analytics Champions, refer to [this document](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1lRMzb0Gwprnyq8VZH5zNRtZK_GxzvmryqgLpaZMQE6g/edit?usp=sharing).

**Owners:** Analytics: Tony Harper / DI: Briton Zurcher / [Canonical list of Analytic Champions](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1R2BNPbdPjHwIAOS7ya-EtQcVcRg-w6abQFI901wiQJE/edit#gid=1973472157)

### SME Program Lead

**DI Program Lead responsibilities:**
- SME Group Program Oversight: Serve as the STO for identifying and organizing SME groups each half.
- SME Group Execution: Oversee the formation and expected operational norms for the SME groups.
- Resource Allocation: Oversee the selection and allocation of DI SMEs.
- "Big Rocks" Execution Plan: Develop and maintain an overall strategy & execution plan.

**Analytics Program Lead responsibilities:**
- Resource Allocation: Organize Analytics SMEs, plugging them into relevant DI SME groups.
- Collaboration Facilitator: Act as a sounding board for the DI SME Program & Group Leads.
- Analytics Advocacy: Ensure the analytics perspective is represented in strategic decisions.

**Shared responsibilities:**
- (stretch) Performance Monitoring: Develop and maintain relevant performance analytics.
- Communication: Act as the primary liaison between SME groups, Analytics champions, and senior leadership.

**Owners:** Analytics: Vibha Virmani / DI: Lana Millson

### SME Group Lead (STO for a domain)

**Responsibilities:**
- SME Group Diversity: Collaborates with Analytics and DI SME Leads to ensure the right mix of SMEs.
- SME Group Performance: Ensures efficient and effective operation towards agreed-upon KRs.
- Escalations: Drives resolution to any escalations/issues/blockers.
- Pivots: Empowers SMEs to re-assess and pivot strategy/priorities/milestones.
- Communication: Acts as the primary liaison between SMEs and the SME Program Leads.
- Cross-Group Collaboration: Actively contributes and participates in regular rhythms.

**Owners:** [Canonical list of DI SME Group Leads](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1R2BNPbdPjHwIAOS7ya-EtQcVcRg-w6abQFI901wiQJE/edit#gid=637275732)

### SME (Subject Matter Expert)

**Responsibilities:**
- Subject Matter Expertise: Perform as an expert resource on the problem domain.
- Productivity: Accountable for delivery of work and key milestones.
- Collaboration: Foster effective collaborations across SME Group peers and groups.

**Examples of Analytics SME Responsibilities:**
- Dogfooding: Drive dogfooding efforts (alpha/beta testing features & bug fixes)
- Adoption: Drive adoption of Data Infrastructure technologies
- Design Reviews: Provide feedback on proposed technical or UX designs
- Diff Reviews: Provide feedback during product development cycles
- Technical Contributions: Contribute code, technical components
- Analyses: Perform data analysis to help inform SME Group decisions
- Surveys: Actively participate in or drive participation in relevant surveys

## Key Processes

**Guiding Principles:**
- Canonically document each step in the process to ensure stateful conversations
- Identify an STO accountable for getting to resolution as early as possible
- Strive for extreme clarity and transparency in all communications
- Frequently share progress for ongoing decisions/issues (e.g. every 3-5 business days)

### 1. Handling Escalations

**Goal:** Establish a clear and efficient escalation path with accountability to ensure timely problem resolution.

**Channels:**

1. Who to contact to initiate an escalation:
   1. First, document the issue to inform triage, representing the facts about the problem and key POCs
   2. If you are an Analytics SME, reach out to your Pillar Champion
   3. If you are a DI SME, reach out to your SME Group Lead
   4. Your SME Group Lead and Pillar Champion will help either resolve the issue locally or guide on how to inform up
   5. If resolved locally, share resolution with your SME Group
2. If further escalation is needed beyond Champions & SME Program Leads, the issue will be brought to the Steerco
   1. Steerco guides how we resolve & inform up to DI / Analytics Leadership

### 2. Plan of Record (POR) Change Management Process

**Goal:** Establish a clear and efficient path to make CRUD updates to DI Roadmaps / HIGH-PRI OKRs to ensure transparent decision making.

**Channels:**

1. DI SME Group Lead documents the proposed change ([Template](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1CrdXqxikRSGqc-GAQLKTRFf6x4QfZ9nkc2orhsC7vDA/edit?usp=sharing)), in collaboration with the DI / Analytics SMEs involved.
2. The DI SME Group Lead informs up to Champions & SME Program Leads.
   - Channel: initiate via the [shared group](https://fb.workplace.com/groups/1716019648922551) & [chat](https://fb.workplace.com/chat/t/6999645450090244).
   - Sign offs are required to close. Where/why Champions and Leads disagree is documented.
   - If can't get to consensus, it becomes an escalation to Steerco.
3. Once resolved, DI SME Group Lead republishes the roadmap / OKRs.

### 3. Change in Analytics SME Allocation Process

**Goal:** Provide clarity on communication path / accountabilities when transitioning Analytic SMEs in/out of SME Groups.

**Channels:**

1. Requester notifies the relevant Pillar Champion and Analytics SME Program Lead (Vibha Virmani).
2. Pillar Champion is accountable for identifying new allocation.
   - Provides update in the [SME STO chat](https://fb.workplace.com/chat/t/6999645450090244)
   - Updates the canonical [SME Group tracking sheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1R2BNPbdPjHwIAOS7ya-EtQcVcRg-w6abQFI901wiQJE/edit?usp=sharing)
3. SME Group Lead is accountable for adding/removing SMEs to relevant workplace groups, chats, meetings.

### 4. Papercut Prioritization and Feedback Loops

**Goal:** Establish a predictable rhythm for getting clarity on the top priority papercuts.

**Channels:**

The first week of the month, each SME Group will conduct a dedicated session on Papercuts led by the SME Group lead, discussing:

1. Papercuts completed or investigated since last session
2. Feedback on the proposed next set of papercuts (adjust priority, align on value)
3. Close the feedback loop on prior fixes or submissions

Participants include all SME Group members + DI Papercut Champions for the problem domain.

- Agenda shared 2 business days in advance by DI Papercut Champions in collaboration with the SME Group Lead.

**Papercuts Resources:** [Papercuts Voter Insights Dashboard](https://www.internalfb.com/intern/unidash/dashboard/analytics_&_di_collaboration/papercuts_voter_insights) / [papercuts process](https://fb.workplace.com/groups/data.infra.team/permalink/1486111788609365/) / [program tracker](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1zikfupCKB7zk9BLBzhDvrjAFrR5c-v0VAgYL8U303_I/edit#gid=468655677)

Note: Papercut priorities are based on the papercut intake/voting process, SME group prioritization, and DI product team recommendation.
