# Analytics Champions

**Project Tag**: `project:analytics_champions`

## Overview

Tony serves as an Analytics Champion in the collaboration between Analytics and Data Infrastructure (DI) teams at Meta. Analytics Champions are the single voice representing their Analytics pillar's needs to the DI organization. They identify and vet SMEs, establish pillar-level processes for SME group execution, advocate for what gets prioritized across Analytics and DI, and drive holistic thinking on end-to-end analytics workflows across multiple domains.

The collaboration operates through a tiered structure: a Steerco, Analytics Champions (one per product pillar), SME Program Leads (one Analytics, one DI), SME Group Leads (one per domain), and SMEs.

## Key Responsibilities

- Act as representative for Analytics pillar's needs to DI
- Identify and vet SMEs for collaboration with DI
- Establish pillar-level processes to ensure SME group execution
- Establish end-to-end analytics lifecycles across multiple domains
- Advocate for and influence what gets prioritized across Analytics and DI (and advocate for DI too, not just Analytics)
- Regularly communicate progress and updates across the pillar
- Participate in semi-annual Analytics Summit with pillar priorities and papercuts
- Proactively identify opportunities to improve the collaboration model

## Key Processes

1. **Escalations** — SMEs escalate to Pillar Champion or SME Group Lead, who resolve locally or escalate to Steerco
2. **Plan of Record Changes** — SME Group Lead documents proposed changes, gets signoff from Champions and Program Leads
3. **SME Allocation Changes** — Pillar Champion accountable for identifying new allocation when SMEs transition
4. **Papercut Prioritization** — Monthly dedicated sessions per SME Group to review, prioritize, and close feedback loops
