# Risk DE 2026 AI Solutions Strategy

*Authors: *Anthony O'Sullivan



---

## Context

Given the rapid expansion and adoption of AI technologies at Meta, it is imperative that the Risk DE team develops a deliberate strategy to adapt to this evolving *way of working*. We must ensure our team is positioned to leverage the AI revolution in a manner that maximizes value for the Risk org, supports Product Groups in adhering to compliance obligations, and fosters the continued growth and development of our Data Engineers.


While there are existing efforts across [Risk](https://fb.workplace.com/groups/197295836397052/permalink/659584703501494/) and [Meta](https://fb.workplace.com/groups/1391035709386611/posts/1394634979026684/) to increase adoption of AI in development workflows, as well as numerous investments[[1](https://fb.workplace.com/groups/dwrgroup/permalink/29527165806905320/);[2](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1pSp1CrUJgYHLCH6DuGRYTZtUAkhobYvWDGaW2XiyMKo/edit?tab=t.0#heading=h.ifue56dv3fv8)] to modernize generic DE challenges, we need to consider where domain expertise from the **Risk DE team can be a strategic advantage** in leveraging AI. Conversely, failure to make the requisite investments now poses significant risks to the long-term impact of the team.



---

## Strategy

While everyone at Meta must adapt to using AI as part of their everyday job, this section outlines targeted investments the Risk DE team should make in 2026 to ensure our expertise is leveraged in shaping future AI driven compliance solutions, and we’re not left behind due to accelerated investments by partner teams.

## Risks to Not Investing

In 2026 H1, it is imperative for Risk DE to intentionally adapt its approach to developing analytical and engineering solutions for compliance, as well as the allocation of bandwidth and resources across roadmap projects. Although these adjustments may present initial challenges, fragmented approaches will incur long-term costs for the team. The following concerns pose a risk to our team's impact, where we must ensure the team is positioned to proactively address.


| **Risk** | **Description** | **Concerns** | **Mitigations** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Data Quality | The state of metric maturity in the Risk org remains quite low, based on this assessment from mid 2025: [Risk DE Measurement Maturity Model](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1gvpb1JDaNmlrTJ_qPyVx396dc5L5W0rmCGNXT5N54M4/edit?brid=7LW__0shxhtOg6AMjaXXLw&fbclid=IwY2xjawOJYSZleHRuA2FlbQIxMQBicmlkETEzaXRmRzZOaUtSaVNGalVOc3J0YwZhcHBfaWQBMAABHm45hGKVgc2aB2b3kFpW5OcgSTjjOtUcROAAIQZZAGdH4lWftJLuNNdFVsvK_aem_2EiYJSLs6K_lV3ZJQwVxjQ&gid=0#gid=0). | Adoption of AI for analytics is dependent on high quality and well governed data as an input. If we’re not intentional about truly addressing metric quality concerns that persist, we hinder the org in being able to leverage our data for decision making. | Risk DE needs to develop an objective measurement maturity model and take intentional OKRs to improve these maturity metrics to a sufficient level across 2026. |

## Risk DE AI Program Structure:

To govern the multiple efforts and projects across Risk DE in 2026, the following program structure is being recommended. 


**STO:**

- Accountable for overall program, cross-org alignment, escalation, and resource allocation.
- Consulted/Informed on execution, risk management, and reporting.
**QB :**

- Responsible for day-to-day execution, risk/issue management, stakeholder communication, and reporting.
- Accountable for ensuring program deliverables are met and issues are escalated appropriately.
- Consulted on resource allocation and strategic decisions.

## **Key Considerations**

- Evolving Role of DE & Tool Adoption
  - Track adoption across different AI tools to identify gaps and opportunities for further investigation and improvement in the context of evolving DE jobs to be done.
  - Leverage dashboards and feedback loops to monitor progress and inform strategy.
  - Establish a baseline for understanding what is good adoption that can be utilized for future goaling.
- Code Quality Assurance
  - Recognize the risk of “AI slop” (low-quality code) as AI code generation increases.
  - Implement robust code quality guardrails to maintain high standards.

## **Meeting Cadence & Expectations**

- Bi-Weekly Status Review Meetings
  - Focused on tracking progress, surfacing risks, and driving accountability.
  - Only off-track items will be discussed to ensure meetings are efficient and action-oriented.
- Roles & Responsibilities
  - Each QB (Quarterback) is responsible for tracking and updating their workstream status per roadmap and milestones.
- Proposed Agenda (Bi-Weekly)
  - Workstream Level Status Sync – 10 min
  - Deep-Dive Topic – 10 min
  - Finalize Action Items & Next Steps – 5 min
