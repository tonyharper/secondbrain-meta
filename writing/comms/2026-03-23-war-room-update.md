# Risk Data AI Enablement War Room — Update

Quick update on where we landed after the war room and what's coming next.

## Progress

The war room produced real results. As of today, we have [5 semantic models ready to deploy or very close](https://risk-de-eval-quality.nest.x2p.facebook.net/), across multiple use cases from Phase 1. We're also making meaningful progress on some of our most difficult domains, including Obligations and Compliance Graph — areas where the data complexity is high and the semantic modeling work is genuinely hard.

Feedback from Stephanie on the results so far has been positive. The format worked: small pods, focused scope, people in the room together solving real problems.

## Tooling Feedback Loops

One thing that came through clearly during the war room: the tooling has gaps, and people are feeling them. I've heard the frustration, and I want to make sure we address it — we need to turn it into something actionable.

We're establishing three feedback loops, each with a dedicated POC:

- **Semantic Model Tooling** — Chris Ventura
- **Eval Tooling** — Sunil Rathode
- **Measurement** — Swetha Singiri

If you hit an issue, have a feature request, or just want to flag something that's slowing you down or that you disagree with, [post it in the AI Enablement group](https://fb.workplace.com/groups/1470044764516758) and tag the relevant POC. We'll work with them to make sure feedback gets triaged and prioritized, not just collected.

We're also opening up the AI Enablement Workplace group as a broader channel for discussion. If you're working on semantic models, evals, or measurement — post there. Questions, blockers, patterns you've found, things that are working. The more visible the work is, the faster we all move.
